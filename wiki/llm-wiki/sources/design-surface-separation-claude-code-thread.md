# Claude Code Thread: Design Alternatives and the Design-Surface Separation

## Core Contribution

The thread converges on a structural reframing of the method: designs are not wiki pages. The wiki is durable knowledge — claim-shaped and evidence-governed — while designs are commitments — workflow-shaped and lifecycle-governed — and therefore belong on a separate surface (dossier directories) governed by a separate schema, with containment rather than the typed-graph model as their organizing principle.

## Key Claims

- The owner's design process starts with a fan-out: several alternative design variations of similar scope, evaluated against each other (trade-offs, implications, obligations, cost, blast radius); the wiki's single-authoritative-document design framing could not accommodate this.
- Designing is inherently a workflow, not information capture and assessment — a structural tension with the wiki's framing (owner claim, ratified by the thread).
- Proposed framing (owner draft, refined in-thread): wiki = durable knowledge capture; designs = workflow-oriented, with the schema capturing the assets and their meaning, not implementing the full workflow.
- Refined framing (agent contribution): the wiki is evidence-governed (authority from sources and independence), a design is lifecycle-governed (authority from state); wiki pages are claim-shaped, designs commitment-shaped.
- The `design` type's misfit was visible in the schema itself: the only type in the authored instigator tier but the descriptive/epistemic family.
- Containment is legitimate for designs once they are their own surface: the dossier holds whatever the design needs; a compartmentalized design directory only conflicted with the wiki's rules while designs were wiki pages.
- The schemas split: wiki and design surfaces share general conventions but their specifics diverge; whether types are parent-scoped (a design-scoped comparison vs a wiki-scoped comparison) is raised and deliberately deferred (owner: "just an idle idea").
- "Designs die into decisions" is rejected as doctrine: a design is not a decision and does not translate into one. Replacement framing: a design changes tense at its terminal lifecycle state, freezing as a durable historical record of intent; the rot problem the old rule solved survives and is handled by the freeze.
- The relationship between a completed design and the wiki (graduation) is deliberately undefined — premature without direct experience; captured as a design obligation rather than resolved.
- Dossiers must not become knowledge traps: durable knowledge produced during design work still belongs in the wiki (reference-direction rule: designs cite wiki freely; wiki links to dossier roots only).
