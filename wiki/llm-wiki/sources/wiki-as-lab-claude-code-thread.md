## Core Contribution

Establishes that this wiki has a second first-class goal alongside its research content: serving as the pilot instance for experimenting with and evolving knowledge-base practice, because the llm-wiki idea is "vague and conceptually directional more than implementation specifics." Derives two consequences: the repo's own operational record is an uncaptured first-party evidence stream, and the lab goal must be written into the contract or agents will keep scoring meta-work as drift.

## Key Claims

1. The owner's motivations for meta-work are threefold: intrinsic interest, deliberate practice-evolution research on a directionally-specified pattern, and enjoyment/accessibility (phone-sized work while multitasking).
2. Under the lab framing, schema evolution is research output, not overhead — the implementation specifics produced (evidence tiers, source isolation, schema layer, lint tooling) are exactly what the llm-wiki concept lacks.
3. The narrowed anti-ceremony warning: meta-findings are only meaningful while a real research workload flows through the wiki.
4. The operational record (log, git history, lint results) is first-party empirical evidence about llm-wiki practice — the only path to non-llm-generated evidence on the vault's own core topic short of external replication.
5. Methodical self-experimentation needs only two cheap habits: a hypothesis-plus-revisit-trigger line when a practice changes, and a verdict assessment citing a snapshotted operational record when one exists.
6. The lab goal passes the non-inferable-details test and must be stated in the contract; its absence caused repeated mis-scoring of intentional work as scope creep — by the reviewing agent itself.
7. Owner constraint: "AI is an abstraction layer removed from me" — meta sources must separate mechanically verifiable observations from agent-narrated interpretation.
